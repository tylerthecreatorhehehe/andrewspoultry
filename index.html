Frequently Asked Questions
What is the minimum order quantity for ready-to-lay chickens?
+
The minimum order quantity for our ready-to-lay chickens typically depends on various factors such as breed availability, shipping logistics, and customer demand.
How are the chickens transported to ensure safety?
+
We prioritize the welfare and safety of our chickens during transportation. Our shipping process involves using specially designed crates or boxes that provide adequate ventilation and space for the chickens to move comfortably.
Is choosing specific breeds or ages of chickens possible?
+
We offer a variety of breeds and ages of ready-to-lay chickens to accommodate the diverse preferences and needs of our customers.
How soon does the chicken lay eggs after order?
+
The time it takes for ready-to-lay chickens to start laying eggs can vary depending on several factors, including breed, age, environmental conditions, and stress levels during transportation and acclimation. Generally, most hens will begin laying eggs around 18-24 weeks of age, but some breeds may start earlier or later.
What type of feed is recommended for the chickens?
+
We recommend a balanced commercial layer feed formulated specifically for laying hens to ensure optimal health and egg production. This feed typically contains essential nutrients such as protein, calcium, vitamins, and minerals necessary for egg development and overall well-being.
